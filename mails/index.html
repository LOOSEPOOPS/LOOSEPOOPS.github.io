2015.04.27 :: loosepoops@gmail.com

Hi Matthew,

As far as space requirements go, we were envisioning the LOOSE POOPS MAKE 1 (the thing we're doing; we've got a name now!) to be a sort of "be-in" or participatory art piece. We're thinking that the second step of the outlined piece would be a good thing to do at Firecreek (where we make collaborative collages of pictures and words) -- we're already getting the photographs ready and setting up the ekphrastics on the backs of them for that. To make this happen, we were thinking that the big table in the main room of Firecreek's venue space would work well -- maybe we could put that in the center of the room and all sit around it, with the pile of photos and craft supplies in the middle of the table? I'm not sure what kind of set-up you're having for other artists that day, but music during the event would be a good idea. I'm not sure re: copyright law on commercial music events, but we can provide a mixtape if necessary, given that information.

In addition to the table in the middle of the room, we're thinking some kind of hanging apparatus would be good for the display of recently finished work -- either on the walls around the venue or directly above the table. Some rough twine with clothespins would be ideal, or we could try to make it look more like a darkroom. Some pointers on the available use of the space would be helpful in this area.

Since the MAKE is going to be participatory, we were thinking that having an extra layer of chairs facing into the table (maybe raised? if possible) for spectators to sit in and suggest juxtapositions / come and have a seat at the table would also be a good idea. Let us know if you think this would be too chaotic.

We should need only two or three outlets (we're mostly an analogue operation), and maybe one mic and possible wall space (see note above). We'll need a big table for about 5-8 people to sit at and as many chairs. We'd like that table and those chairs to be on the floor of the venue (stages have a power dynamic we're not going for in our MAKE). One more question is: what about signage? Will a lot of it be provided, or should we make our own? Are T-Shirts a good idea?

We look forward to your reply. We're super pumped about May 23 and the rest of the SSOO event!

Hugs,
Case Duckworth
and the LOOSE POOPS team
